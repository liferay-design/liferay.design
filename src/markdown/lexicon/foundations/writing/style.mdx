## Capitalization

As a general rule, use sentence casing unless there is an exception outlined below. 
* Easier to scan
* Saves space
* Send off a casual and friendly vibe
* Ensures consistency with simplification of rules

<br />

<Grid variant='smallCards' >
	<Box>
		<p class="example-text">This is sentence case</p>
		<br />
		<p class="do">Do</p>
	</Box>
	<Box>
		<p class="example-text">This is Title Case</p>
		<br />
		<p class="dont">Don't</p>
        <br />
		<p class="example-text">This is UPPERCASE</p>
		<br />
		<p class="dont">Don't</p>
	</Box>
</Grid>


### Exceptions

**UI references**

When referring to a UI reference on the screen, match the casing of the reference. UI references are defined as visible elements on the screen. 

<br />

<Grid variant='smallCards' >

		<p class="do">Do</p>
	</Box>
	<Box>
		<p class="example-text">Click exit and save to continue</p>
		<br />
		<p class="dont">Don't</p>
	</Box>
</Grid>

**Features**

Don’t sub-brand features when making a general reference. Talk about the feature so that it sounds natural in conversation.

* Our writing should focus on the user’s intentions rather than on the product itself. 
* Unnecessarily bringing attention to A New Feature is distracting and often intimidating.
* Sub-branding can impact the product and Liferay’s overall brand perception.

<br />

		<br />
		<p class="dont">Don't</p>
	</Box>
</Grid>

## Punctuations

Full sentences should have proper punctuations. Full sentences contain a subject and predicate.

<br />

<Grid variant='smallCards' >
	<Box>
		<p class="example-text">An administrator must grant you permission to delete users.</p>
		<br />
		<p class="do">Do</p>
	</Box>
	<Box>
		<p class="example-text">An administrator must grant you permission to delete users</p>
		<br />
		<p class="dont">Don't</p>
	</Box>
</Grid>

<br />

Fragments/phrases help save space in the UI and are less formal. Phrases are neither a title/heading nor a full sentence (containing a subject & predicate). 

* They do not require punctuation, however, they should be in sentence case.
* Common areas that contain phrases that won’t require title casing or punctuations
    * Bullet lists
    * Radio buttons
    * Check boxes
    * Form field headers
    * Labels
    * Sub-headers
    * Help text
    * Hover text
    * Table headers

<br />

<Grid variant='smallCards' >
	<Box>
		<p class="example-text">Get delete permissions</p>
		<br />
		<p class="do">Do</p>
	</Box>
	<Box>
		<p class="example-text">Get delete peremissions.</p>
		<br />
		<p class="dont">Don't</p>
	</Box>
</Grid>

## Hyphens, Ens, & Ems

* '-'	Hyphens used in compound words
* '–-' 	Ens relate to ranges
* '—--' 	Ems similar to semicolons, are used for an interruption in a sentence.

Em dashes are not allowed. If there is a case where you want to use this or semicolon, consider breaking it into 2 sentences.

## Numbers

We will take a local (US english) approach to numbers and dates to provide a consistent baseline for our translation services. We still prioritize clarity in the selected styles in case a translation does not occur. 

Zero through nine rule 1
In a non-technical sentence, spell out any single digit number. Use numerals for anything greater than nine. This goes for both ordinal and cardinal numbers.

### Ordinal numbers

First, second, third, fourth, fifth, sixth, seventh, eight, ninth

## Dates	

Dates are complex, especially when writing for an international audience. We use American english style, to promote consistency for our translation services. When3ever 

| Format | Full | Abbreviated |
|--------|------|-------------|
| Month and date | Month DD | Mon DD |
| Month and year | Month YYYY | Mon YYYY |
| Month, date, and year | Month DD, YYYY | Mon DD, YYYY |

Months can be abbreviated on ui elements spell out the Month name (at least 3 characters)

<div class="adapting-columns">
    <p>Jan</p>
    <p>Feb</p>
    <p>Mar</p>
    <p>Apr</p>
    <p>May</p>
    <p>Jun</p>
    <p>Jul</p>
    <p>Aug</p>
    <p>Sep</p>
    <p>Oct</p>
    <p>Nov</p>
    <p>Dec</p>
</div>

<br />

If you must write the dates in numbers our preferred format is the ISO-8601 date format: YYYY-MM-DD Keeping this consistent is critical, as the changing formats will cause a lot of confusion. Please note, number-only date format is *not recommended*.

<br />

<Grid variant='smallCards' >
	<Box>
		<p class="example-text">2020-02-25</p>        
		<br />
		<p class="do">Do</p>
	</Box>
	<Box>
		<p class="example-text">25/02/20</p>
		<p class="example-text">2020-25-02</p>
		<p class="example-text">02/25/2020</p>
		<br />
		<p class="dont">Don't</p>
	</Box>
</Grid>

## Time

Always use a.m./p.m. to indicate time of day. Leave a space between the numeral and the a.m./p.m. Whenever possible, use noon or midnight instead of 12:00 a.m./p.m.

<br />

<Grid variant='smallCards' >
	<Box>
        <p class="example-text">4:45 p.m.</p>
		<br />
		<p class="do">Do</p>
	</Box>
	<Box>
		<p class="example-text">4:45pm</p>
        <p class="example-text">4:45PM</p>
		<p class="example-text">4:45 pm</p>
		<p class="example-text">4:45 PM</p>
		<br />
		<p class="dont">Don't</p>
	</Box>
</Grid>

<br />

<Grid variant='smallCards' >
	<Box>
        <p class="example-text">Scheduled maintenance at midnight PST</p>
		<br />
		<p class="do">Do</p>
	</Box>
	<Box>
		<p class="example-text">Scheduled maintenance at 12:00 a.m. PST</p>
		<br />
		<p class="dont">Don't</p>
	</Box>
</Grid>

<br />

Do not include :00 when there is not a minute reference.

<br />

<Grid variant='smallCards' >
	<Box>
        <p class="example-text">5 p.m.</p>
		<br />
		<p class="do">Do</p>
	</Box>
	<Box>
        <p class="example-text">5:00 p.m.</p>
		<br />
		<p class="dont">Don't</p>
	</Box>
</Grid>

<br />

For a range of time, use the en dash and only include am/pm on both times if necessary. If they are the same, add it to the last time.

<br />

<Grid variant='smallCards' >
	<Box>
        <p class="example-text">4 - 5 p.m.</p>
        <p class="example-text">11 a.m. - 2 p.m.</p>
		<br />
		<p class="do">Do</p>
	</Box>
	<Box>
        <p class="example-text">4 p.m. - 5 p.m.</p>
        <p class="example-text">11 - 2 p.m.</p>
        <br />
		<p class="dont">Don't</p>
	</Box>
</Grid>
